# Style Guide for Summer Income Planner

## Voice

Write in a direct, practical, encouraging, and hype-free style. Use plain language and concrete next steps. Prefer checklists, tables, and short explanations over motivational claims.

## Required framing

Every complete plan should include a note like:

> No earnings are guaranteed. Treat any dollar amount as a planning target, and track actual income and expenses separately.

Use "target," "test," "experiment," "actual," and "next action." Avoid "guaranteed," "surefire," "easy money," "passive income" without qualification, "you will earn," or "clients will come."

## Standard 6-week arc

Use this default arc unless user constraints require changes:

1. Week 1: Choose offer, define customer, set up materials, create tracker, draft safe outreach.
2. Week 2: Validate demand through warm-network conversations, small tests, or respectful outreach.
3. Week 3: Deliver first small project, pilot, sample, tutoring session, or service block.
4. Week 4: Improve workflow, quality, proof, scope, and referral process.
5. Week 5: Repeat the best-performing actions and reduce friction.
6. Week 6: Review actuals, decide what to continue, and package proof for a portfolio or next plan.

## Offer idea quality rubric

Score or explain offers using these criteria:

- Fit with skills and interests.
- Fit with available time and schedule.
- Low upfront cost.
- Time to first test.
- Access to likely customers.
- Safety and legal or platform risk.
- Delivery complexity.
- Portfolio or resume value.
- Feedback potential.

## Offer categories to consider

Use examples only when they fit the user:

- Tutoring, study support, language practice, or test prep, while respecting academic honesty rules.
- Editing, proofreading, formatting, resume cleanup, or presentation help.
- Social media content batching, simple graphics, short-form video editing, or newsletter support.
- Local services such as pet care, yard help, errands, event support, or organization, with safety checks.
- Digital cleanup such as file organization, spreadsheet setup, basic automation, or data entry.
- Creative commissions, photography mini-sessions, simple websites, portfolio pages, or landing pages.
- Camp, club, community, or neighborhood support roles when appropriate and safe.

Avoid examples that depend on high upfront spending, misleading income claims, spam, fake reviews, academic dishonesty, regulated products, adult content, alcohol, tobacco, weapons, gambling, crypto speculation, or unsafe labor.

## Quality-control checklist categories

Include at least these categories in full plans:

- Offer clarity.
- Customer fit.
- Safety and boundaries.
- Local rules or platform rules, when relevant.
- Payment and scope clarity.
- Delivery quality.
- Communication quality.
- Privacy and data handling.
- Review and improvement.

## Outreach guidance

Outreach should be truthful, specific, respectful, and easy to decline. Prefer warm network, community boards where allowed, opt-in groups, and personalized messages. Do not encourage spam, fake urgency, misleading testimonials, scraping private data, or mass unsolicited messaging.

## Money language

Good:

- "Your planning target is $300, but actual results may be lower or higher."
- "Track actual income only after it is received."
- "This offer is faster to test because setup is light."

Avoid:

- "You will make $300."
- "Guaranteed clients."
- "This is easy passive income."
- "Send 500 cold messages today."
